      <h1>WIPP Update</h1>
        <h6 align="left"><strong>December 30, 2016</strong><br />
          <br />
        </h6>
        <p align="left"><span style="font-size: small"><strong>Jeff Carswell Selected as CBFO Deputy Manager</strong></span></p>
        <p align="center"><img src="Images/Jeff_Carswell_small.jpg" width="160" height="200" alt="carswell" style="float:right" hspace="12" />Jeffrey M. Carswell has been selected as Deputy Manager, Carlsbad Field Office (CBFO) and also  appointed to the Senior Executive Service. Carswell has been at CBFO since  February 2015 as the Senior Technical Safety Manager.  </p>
<p>He recently  led a nuclear safety team to develop the upgraded Documented Safety Analysis  (DSA) and Technical Safety Requirements (TSR) outlining the new safety  parameters for operations at WIPP.  He is  also the Acting National Transuranic (TRU) Program Recovery Manager, working  across the DOE complex to implement improvements in the program criteria for  accepting TRU waste at WIPP.<br />
  <br />
  &ldquo;It gives me  great pleasure to announce Jeff&rsquo;s selection as our new Deputy Manager,&rdquo; said  Todd Shrader, Manager CBFO.  &ldquo;Jeff brings  a wealth of experience from the Navy&rsquo;s nuclear submarine program, several  positions at the DOE&rsquo;s Savannah River Site and as a recognized leader in  nuclear safety. I look forward to having Jeff as part of the CBFO and WIPP  leadership team.&rdquo;</p>
<p>&ldquo;I would  also like to thank Sean Dunagan for serving as our Acting Deputy Manager for  the last year,&rdquo; Shrader said. </p>
<p>In 1987,  Jeff received a commission in the U. S. Navy and attended the Navy&rsquo;s Nuclear  Power School followed by tours onboard two ballistic missile submarines in  Kings Bay, Georgia.  Upon leaving active  duty, he initially worked in the semiconductor industry before relocating to  Savannah River Site (SRS) in Aiken, South Carolina, where he held a variety of  contractor nuclear safety and engineering positions.  </p>
<p>In 2009,  Jeff joined the Department as a Facility Representative, responsible for  oversight of the HB-Line facility at SRS. He then accepted a position as a  Program Manager at the Savannah River National Laboratory (SRNL) overseeing  Homeland Defense, National Security, and non-proliferation related programs at  the laboratory. In 2012, Jeff returned to the SRS contractor as the Safety  Basis Regulating Authority for the H-Canyon facility. He returned to federal  service at CBFO in February 2015.  </p>
<p>Jeff  continues serving in the Navy Reserve as a Captain, assigned to Commander,  Naval Forces Europe, Naval Forces Africa, Sixth Fleet Detachment 802 in  Atlanta, Georgia, holding senior leadership positions as the Chief of Staff and  Executive Assistant. </p>
<p>A Michigan  native, he received a Bachelor of Science degree in Chemistry from Wayne State  University in Detroit, and  holds a  Master&rsquo;s in Business Administration from Brenau University.  </p>
